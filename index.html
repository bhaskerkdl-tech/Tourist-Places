Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is considered to be the greatest architectural achievement in the whole range of Indo-Islamic architecture. Its recognised architectonic beauty has a rhythmic combination of solids and voids, concave and convex and light shadow; such as arches and domes further increases the aesthetic aspect. Not a piece of architecture, as other buildings are, but the proud passions of an emperor’s love wrought in living stones.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the most sacred pilgrimage site in Sikhism, located in the heart of Amritsar, Punjab. This architectural masterpiece, standing in the center of the holy Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar), was originally designed by Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, and its foundation stone was laid by the Muslim Sufi saint Mian Mir in 1588 to symbolize interfaith harmony. The temple’s unique structure, featuring a 24-karat gold-leaf-covered dome and walls of white marble, was significantly embellished by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in 1830, giving it its iconic "Golden" appearance. Built on a level lower than the surrounding city, the temple requires visitors to descend steps to enter, a design choice intended to reflect humility. It boasts four entrances facing each cardinal direction, signifying that it is open to people of all faiths, castes, and backgrounds. Beyond its physical beauty, the temple complex houses the world’s largest community kitchen, the Guru Ram Das Langar, which serves free vegetarian meals to approximately 100,000 people daily, embodying the Sikh principles of selfless service and universal equality.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a breathtaking historical landmark and royal residence located in the heart of Mysuru, Karnataka. Designed by the British architect Henry Irwin and completed in 1912, this three-story granite structure is a premier example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, blending Hindu, Islamic, Rajput, and Gothic styles. It served as the official seat of the Wadiyar dynasty, who ruled the Kingdom of Mysore for over six centuries. The palace is renowned for its opulent interiors, featuring the magnificent Durbar Hall with its ornate ceilings, the octagonal Kalyana Mantapa (marriage pavilion) with its multi-hued stained-glass dome, and the jewel-encrusted Golden Throne displayed only during the grand Dasara festival. A defining feature of the palace is its evening illumination; on Sundays, public holidays, and during Dasara, the entire structure is transformed by nearly 100,000 electric bulbs, making it one of the most visited monuments in India.

Varanasi Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, also known as the Golden Temple of Varanasi, is one of the most sacred Hindu shrines and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located on the western bank of the holy river Ganga, it is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest of Shiva temples, and is believed to offer liberation (moksha) to those who visit. The current structure was built in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore, while its iconic golden spires and domes were later covered with approximately 1,000 kg of gold donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh of Punjab in 1835. The temple features a unique Nagara style of architecture with three distinct sections: a main spire, a golden dome, and a second golden spire topped with a flag and trident. Within its complex lies the Gyan Vapi (Well of Knowledge), where the original Shiva Lingam was reportedly hidden during historical invasions. Recently, the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor has transformed the area, creating a spacious and direct connection between the ancient temple and the river ghats for pilgrims.
